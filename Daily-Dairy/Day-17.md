Training day 17 
Smart Student Mentor 
Introduction 
Day 17 of the Smart Student Mentor Project was dedicated to data collection and 
requirement analysis. This phase is one of the most important stages in software 
development, as it helps in understanding what the system is expected to do and 
what resources are required for successful implementation. The aim of this day’s 
work was to gather relevant academic data and analyze the functional and non
functional requirements of the Smart Student Mentor system. 
A clear understanding of student needs and system expectations ensures that the 
final product is useful, efficient, and user-friendly. This phase helped in defining 
the scope and direction of the project. 
Data Collection 
Data collection focused on gathering academic and educational resources that are 
essential for building an intelligent mentor system. Various sources of 
information were studied, including textbooks, syllabus documents, class notes, 
reference books, and online educational materials. These resources were analyzed 
to identify common topics, frequently asked questions, and important concepts 
that students usually find difficult. 
Special attention was given to syllabus-oriented content so that the system can 
provide accurate and relevant answers. The collected data was reviewed to ensure 
clarity, correctness, and consistency. This step is crucial as the quality of data 
directly affects the performance of the Smart Student Mentor system. 
The collected information was categorized based on subjects, topics, and 
difficulty levels. This structured approach helps the system deliver personalized 
learning support to students and improves the overall learning experience. 
Requirement Analysis 
Requirement analysis involves identifying the needs and expectations of users 
and defining how the system should function. In this project, the primary users 
are students who seek academic guidance. 
Functional Requirements: 
• The system should accept student queries in text form. 
• It should understand academic questions and provide accurate 
explanations. 
• The system should guide students through concepts in a simple manner. 
• It should support continuous interaction between students and the mentor 
system. 
Non-Functional Requirements: 
• The system should provide fast and accurate responses. 
• It should be reliable and available at all times. 
• The interface should be simple and easy to use. 
• The system should be scalable to handle multiple users. 
This analysis ensured that both user needs and technical constraints were 
considered before moving to the design phase. 
Importance of Requirement Analysis 
Requirement analysis plays a key role in avoiding future errors and 
misunderstandings during development. By clearly defining system requirements 
at an early stage, the chances of rework and system failure are reduced. It also 
helps developers stay focused on project goals and ensures that the system meets 
user expectations. 
For the Smart Student Mentor Project, this phase helped in aligning the system’s 
capabilities with student learning objectives. 
Conclusion 
Day 17 marked an important milestone in the Smart Student Mentor Project. The 
data collection and requirement analysis activities provided a strong foundation 
for the next stages of development. By identifying relevant academic data and 
clearly defining system requirements, the project is now ready to proceed to 
system design and implementation. This phase ensured that the Smart Student 
Mentor system would be effective, reliable, and beneficial for students.
